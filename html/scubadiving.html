Scubadiving in Fiji

Nicknamed both “The Soft Coral Capital of the World” and “The Best Shark Diving Location on Earth,” Fiji has rightfully earned its reputation as a premier dive destination. The powerful currents sweeping through the outer reefs deliver nutrient-rich waters from across the Pacific, attracting an extraordinary variety of marine life—from tiny, colorful macro creatures to breathtaking pelagic species that glide through the deep blue. Home to over 390 coral species and approximately 1,200 species of fish, Fiji offers an underwater paradise for divers and marine enthusiasts alike. The diving here ranges from dramatic wall dives and swim-throughs to caverns, canyons, and thrilling blue-water encounters featuring bull sharks, wahoo, and even the occasional manta ray or whale shark. Diving in Fiji isn't just an adventure—it's an immersion into one of the ocean's most vibrant and awe-inspiring ecosystems.
