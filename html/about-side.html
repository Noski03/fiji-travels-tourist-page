The Fiji islands

Discover Fiji: Tropical Paradise in the South Pacific

Fiji is this amazing island country in the Pacific, sitting about 2,000 km north of New Zealand. It's like something from a postcard—white sand, clear lagoons, and really lush landscapes.

Around a million people live there, mostly on the main island, Viti Levu. That's where Suva, the capital city, is located.

The culture is a cool combo of Melanesian, Polynesian, and Indian. People speak Fijian, English, and Hindi. The locals are known for being super welcoming and friendly. Fiji's national motto is Rerevaka na Kalou ka Doka na Tui, which translates to 'Fear God and Honour the King/Queen'. They've had their democracy back since 2014 after being under military rule for a bit.

Nature and Climate

The islands have everything from coral reefs to green valleys and dense jungles. Mount Tomanivi is the tallest spot, at 1,323 metres. The weather is nice and warm all year round. Expect around 23 °C in winter (July-August) and about 27 °C in summer (January-March). It does rain some about 3,200 mm a year, and they do get hit by cyclones sometimes.

Unique Wildlife and National

Fiji has some interesting animals. Bats are the only local land mammals, and there's also the Fiji iguana—a bright green lizard that hangs out in trees and exists only there. They are trying to protect them through local efforts. The national flower is the red Tagimoucia, which only grows on Taveuni, and the Collared Lory (Kula) is the national bird, loved because it is very colourful.

Fiji history

One interesting spot is the Momi Bay Gun Battery, built in World War II for defense. Naihehe Caves are another place where a local tribe hid out way back when, and there are tons of old stories about them. The Garden of the Sleeping Giant, close to Nadi, presents Fiji's plants and gives you a bit of background on the islands' past. Fiji became independent from Britain on October 10, 1970, they celebrate Fiji Day every year.
