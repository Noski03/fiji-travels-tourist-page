Climbing in Fiji

The country's three most popular and rewarding climbs

Mount Tomanivi (formerly Mount Victoria) is Fiji's highest peak at 1,324 meters. Starting near Navai village, the trail winds through dense cloud forest with native plants and birds. The hike is steep and muddy, but the summit rewards climbers with sweeping views of the islands and highlands—perfect for experienced hikers.

Mount Koroyanitu (Mount Evans), at 1,195 meters, lies within Koroyanitu Heritage Park between Nadi and Lautoka. Trails mix challenging climbs with gentler forest walks, passing local villages and offering views of the Mamanuca and Yasawa Islands on clear days.

Mount Batilamu (“The Sleeping Giant”) rises to 1,110 meters. Its trail from Abaca Village is moderately difficult, taking about five hours round trip. Hikers enjoy waterfalls, swimming holes, and rich birdlife, with the option to stay overnight in a hut near the summit.
